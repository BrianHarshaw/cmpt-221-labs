Water

One thing I learned to love more as I have grown up is water. Although it is plain, with no flavor or color to it, drinking water is essential to one's health. After being told off by my doctor one too many times for being dehydrated I started hydrating properly and have never looked back.

Soccer (Football)

I used to play soccer all the time when I was in elementary school, and nowadays I meet my soccer quota by watching match highlights and FIFA videos. Although sitting at home and watching videos is not nearly as healthy as actually playing the sport, I am gaining more than enough knowledge about it to meet my needs. The name still confuses me though.

Video Games

Ever since I was little I have been playing video games, from the Nintendo 64 to the original Xbox all the way to current gen consoles. It is a convenient and fun way to hang out with friends when you can't meet up in person, and now the only thing that keeps me from playing is the responsibilities that keep piling on as I have grown up.

Music

Although I definitely do not own nor use a record player, music is a constant in my everyday life. Listening to music when walking to class, when doing homework, or whenever is a great way to keep an elevated mood and stay in touch with yourself. Without music life would be so boring in those in-between moments.

Sleep

Sleep is essential to all people, and a luxury to college students nationwide. Gone are the days of waking up at 6 am to go to school, although going to sleep at 6 am has become an all too common occurrence.

Cold Seasons

It may just be because I have grown up in New England, but nonetheless the cold days of the winter and fall are the best of the year. Wearing hoodies, sweatpants, and other comfortable variations of cloting is only possible during this time of the year, while summer is just hell.
